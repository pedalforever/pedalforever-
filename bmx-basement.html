The BMX Basement

Built under a 1900 house, hidden behind doors, stairs, bulkhead entrances, old tools, survivor bikes, lowriders, parts drawers, stickers, chrome baths, and questionable promises that this is definitely “the last bike.”

If the lights go out and the door shuts, this page changes. The bikes know who came home last. The basement always keeps score.

The Stairs

The official entrance to the underground. Every bike that comes down these stairs gets judged before the kickstand even touches concrete.

The Bulkhead

The emergency delivery route for bikes that were absolutely not supposed to come home today. Daylight ends at the doors. The collection begins below.

The Chrome Corner

Where rusty parts go into the tub sink and come back acting like they still have factory paperwork. Oxalic acid, patience, and blind faith.

The Parts Graveyard

Drawers, boxes, mystery stems, oddball bolts, tires, pads, seats, grips, and the one washer Joey Topcap swears belongs to something important.

The Survivor Row

Bikes that stayed original because sometimes the scratches are the story. Barn finds, basement finds, and bikes that already lived a whole life.

The Back Room

Nobody really knows what is back there. Pete says he does. That is why nobody trusts the back room.

Current Basement Artifact

This is where one bike, frame, part, sticker, tool, or strange basement object gets pulled out of the pile and treated like evidence.

Some of it is rare. Some of it is weird. Some of it was saved from trash, barns, rivers, garages, sheds, attics, and bad decisions.

“Guys, be nice. This one’s had a rough life.”

Basement Rules

Do not ask how many bikes are down here.
Do not move anything Joey said was “sorted.”
Do not leave Pete alone with anodized parts.
If Tom mentions sponsorship, nod politely.
If the tires roll by themselves, pretend you didn’t see it.
If someone upstairs asks, this bike was already here.
